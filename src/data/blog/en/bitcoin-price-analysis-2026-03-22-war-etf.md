## Introduction  
Bitcoin slipped to $68,640 this week, a near‑3% drop driven by the escalating U.S.–Israel–Iran conflict and a massive wave of ETF outflows. In this Bitcoin price analysis we break down the macro backdrop, capital flows, and technical signals, and we outline actionable strategies for traders on Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, and Bybit.  

## What’s Happening  

- **Price action**: Binance data shows a 24‑hour high of $71,100.94 and a low of $68,110.55, closing at $68,640 (‑2.95%). Major altcoins—Ethereum, BNB, SOL—also fell 2.5%‑4% in the same period.  
- **ETF outflows**: CoinTelegraph reported that Bitcoin spot ETFs recorded a net outflow of roughly $3 billion over the last week, the biggest weekly exodus since 2024. Large U.S. fund managers cite rising geopolitical tension and liquidity risk as reasons for pulling back from volatile assets.  
- **War dynamics**: The fourth week of U.S.–Israeli air strikes on Iran has rattled energy markets, lifted the dollar index above 105, and boosted demand for traditional safe‑haven assets, relegating crypto to a risk‑off tier.  

## Why It Matters  

1. **Direct capital hit**: ETFs are the main conduit for institutional Bitcoin exposure. Net outflows instantly reduce buying pressure and amplify downside risk.  
2. **Higher macro risk premium**: A stronger dollar and rising U.S. Treasury yields shift investor preferences toward cash and gold, eroding Bitcoin’s “digital gold” narrative.  
3. **Technical support test**: The $68,000 zone—aligned with the 50‑day moving average—acts as a key support. A break below could trigger broader sell‑offs.  

## Bullish Catalysts  

- **Technical support**: Historically, the $68,000 area has acted as a springboard. If price stabilises and buying resumes, a short‑term bounce is plausible.  
- **Sustained long‑term demand**: Global crypto payments, DeFi growth, and continued institutional reserve accumulation—particularly from Asian custodians—keep underlying demand intact.  
- **Conflict de‑escalation**: Any cease‑fire or diplomatic progress would lower risk premiums, potentially channeling capital back into high‑beta assets like Bitcoin.  

## Bearish Pressures  

- **Ongoing ETF outflows**: Continued withdrawals could widen the demand gap, pushing price toward the $66,000 psychological barrier.  
- **Dollar strength**: Persistent Fed tightening expectations keep the dollar index climbing, further dimming Bitcoin’s appeal against USD.  
- **Deteriorating sentiment**: Heightened global economic uncertainty nudges investors toward cash or gold, starving Bitcoin of fresh buying interest.  

## Trading Recommendations  

1. **Short‑term play**: Place buy limit orders around $68,000 on Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, or Bybit. Set a stop‑loss just below $66,500. If the level holds and volume picks up, consider scaling in.  
2. **Mid‑to‑long‑term allocation**: Risk‑tolerant investors may dollar‑cost average during the current pull‑back, targeting $72,000‑$75,000 as a post‑conflict recovery range.  
3. **Diversify**: Pair Bitcoin exposure with Ethereum, BNB, or other ecosystem tokens to mitigate single‑asset volatility.  

## Risk Disclaimer  

- **Geopolitical escalation**: A wider war could intensify safe‑haven flows, driving Bitcoin lower.  
- **Regulatory headwinds**: Ongoing SEC scrutiny of crypto ETFs may tighten rules, further restricting inflows.  
- **Technical breakdown**: A slip below $66,000 could trigger cascading stop‑loss orders, accelerating the decline.  

## Bottom Line  

Bitcoin is currently squeezed by three forces: war‑driven risk aversion, a soaring dollar, and sizable ETF outflows. While short‑term downside risk is real, the $68,000 support remains intact, and any easing of macro tension could spark a rebound into the $68,000‑$72,000 band. Keep a close eye on ETF net flow data, the dollar index, and conflict developments, and adjust positions with disciplined stop‑losses.
